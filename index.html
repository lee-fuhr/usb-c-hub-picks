How we choose our picks

We don’t run our own timed trials or tear every hub apart in a lab. Instead, we build our picks from three sources: the reputation of the manufacturer (long track records in consumer electronics), the collective feedback of owners who post detailed, long-term reviews on major retail and forum sites, and the coverage of established, hands-on review outlets that do test data-transfer speeds, power delivery stability, and compatibility across devices. What actually breaks or annoys people over time? Port failure is the most common complaint – especially HDMI ports that stop recognizing monitors or USB-A ports that become loose. We put extra weight on hubs that owners report still work perfectly after a year or more of daily use, and we avoid models with frequent complaints about overheating, flaky video output, or underpowered charging.
