Modern laptops – especially thin-and-light models – often come with just one or two USB-C ports. That’s fine for charging, but it leaves you scrambling when you need to connect a monitor, mouse, external drive, and Ethernet all at once. A good USB-C hub turns a single port into a full workstation in seconds. With so many options on the market, the challenge is finding the one that matches your specific needs without wasting money on ports you’ll never use. When shopping for a hub, start by listing the devices you plug in regularly: do you need HDMI, DisplayPort, or both? How many USB-A devices do you have? Are you transferring large files that benefit from faster USB 3.2 or Thunderbolt speeds? Also consider whether the hub supports power delivery passthrough so your laptop stays charged, and check the build quality – a metal hub dissipates heat better and feels more durable.
